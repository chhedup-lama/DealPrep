# AE interview notes

*Notes from five short conversations with AEs across the team. Recorded for the Internal AI PM team's GTM discovery work · 2026-Q2*

Raw notes, lightly cleaned. Quotes are paraphrased where I missed a word, marked verbatim where I caught it exactly.

---

## Lena Köhler — Senior AE, MM, EMEA · 4 years tenure

Lena owns ~35 accounts, mostly DACH and Iberia, mid-market mix of customers and prospects. Spoke to her on a Tuesday between back-to-back calls.

> "On any given day I have 2–3 calls I need to prep for. The good prep is maybe 30 minutes per call. The honest prep is more like 10. And in those 10 minutes I'm bouncing between Salesforce, Gong, the Drive folder where the battlecards live, and sometimes a Slack thread."

> "The thing that kills me is the renewal where it's quietly slipping. I won't notice unless I look at the activity log carefully, and I don't always look. By the time I notice, we're three weeks from renewal."

When I asked what she'd want an assistant to do, she paused for a long time.

> "I think the honest answer is: tell me what I'd miss. I'm OK on the obvious stuff — the account is in stage X, the opp is worth Y. I want the thing my brain isn't catching. The pattern across three weeks of activity. The thing the support tickets are saying that the activity log isn't."

She mentioned a specific deal — wouldn't share the name — where the deal had been stuck for "nearly three months" and she only realised the pattern because her manager flagged it on a forecast call. "By then it was too late to do anything about it."

> "I would actually trust an AI to tell me what's interesting about an account. I don't think I'd trust it to tell me what to *say*. Big difference."

She also mentioned she works late and on weekends and prep is one of the things she'd genuinely offload if she could.

## Marcus Byrne — Senior AE, ENT, UK & Ireland · 7 years tenure

Marcus is one of the more sceptical AEs about new tooling. He came in with arms folded, metaphorically.

> "Look, my prep ritual is mine. I read the activity log, I check usage trends if it's a customer, I look at any open tickets, I check my notes from the last call. I'm not handing that off."

When I pushed on whether AI could help any part of that, he softened a little.

> "Could it summarise the last 20 emails? Maybe. Would that save me 3 minutes? Probably. Would I rather just have a one-line answer to 'what changed since I last looked at this account' than scroll through 20 emails? Yeah, that I'd take."

He had a specific gripe about competitive intelligence:

> "The battlecards are great. I read them once. After that I don't open them. If somebody could just *tell me* 'this customer is also evaluating HiBob and here's the relevant page from the battlecard' in the moment, that I'd actually use."

He doesn't like the term "AI assistant" — said it makes him think of chatbots that don't work. Worth noting for naming / positioning, not necessarily for product.

> "Verbatim: I don't want a tool that tries to be smart for me. I want a tool that gets me to the right information faster."

## Sofia Alvarez — AE, MM, UKI · 18 months tenure

Sofia joined from a customer success role at a competitor. Hungry, has good instincts but is still learning the playbook.

> "Honestly the thing I struggle with most is knowing the playbook well enough to apply it in the moment. There's a sales playbook, an ICP doc, ten battlecards. I read them all when I joined. Now I have to remember what's in them while I'm on a call."

> "Verbatim: I'd love if I could just ask 'what does the discovery playbook say about evaluating a deal stuck in qualification?' and get the answer without going to find the doc."

She talked about a recent discovery call where she'd missed asking a key qualifying question.

> "The frame for that call was clear. I knew what I needed to ask. I just forgot one of the questions because I was tracking the conversation. If something had nudged me at the right time — even a checklist — I'd have caught it."

She was the only AE who specifically asked about case studies / references:

> "When I'm prepping, the thing I almost always want is 'who else have we sold to that looks like this customer.' I'm always asking a colleague that question. I'd love to ask a tool instead."

When I asked about trust she said something I thought was sharp:

> "If a tool tells me a wrong fact about a customer, I lose three months of trust in it. So I'd rather it told me less and was right than told me more and was wrong sometimes."

## Thomas Weber — AE, MM, DACH · 3 years tenure

Thomas is the AE I had hoped to interview last but Lena pulled him into a call so he gave me 20 minutes only.

> "Most of my prep is about remembering. I know what's in the account, I knew last week. But I work on 30 accounts. I don't hold them all in my head."

When I asked what an ideal pre-call brief would look like:

> "Two lines. 'Here's what's changed since last time you looked. Here's what's worth bringing up on the call.' That's it. Anything longer I'm going to skim."

He was specific about *not* wanting summaries of his own notes:

> "I trust my own notes. I don't need an AI to summarise what I wrote a week ago. I'll read it. What I need is the stuff I *didn't* write down — the support ticket I missed, the product usage drop last month, the activity from the SDR that touched the account."

He also flagged something about pricing:

> "If you build something that talks about pricing, please don't let it just spit out the cheat sheet. Pricing is contextual. The cheat sheet is a starting point. You ask in a specific deal context, you need a specific answer, not the cheat sheet pasted in."

## Ines Dubois — Senior AE, ENT, France · 6 years tenure

Ines covers enterprise. Different shape — fewer accounts, much bigger deals, much longer cycles, more stakeholders per deal.

> "My problem isn't 'I don't have time to prep.' I prep extensively. My problem is keeping the context fresh in my head across a six-month cycle with 12 stakeholders."

She talked about her current workflow which is a personal Notion database per account.

> "I keep my own notes. Nobody else's notes are in a structure that matches how I think. Maybe an AI could help me re-find things in my own notes faster — that I'd take. Summarising activity for me is less useful, because I already know what happened."

She was clear that for enterprise the value is different:

> "The mid-market AEs want help getting context fast. I want help connecting dots across stakeholders. Different problem. Don't try to build one thing for both of us."

> "Verbatim: I work on five accounts at a time. I know everything about all five. Don't tell me what I already know."

She brought up a recent loss to internal build:

> "We lost a deal because we never got the CIO into the room. If something had told me, two months earlier, 'you have no IT/engineering contact at this account', I'd have done something about it. The data was there. Nobody surfaced it."

## Patterns I noticed (rough)

Not a synthesis, just patterns I saw across the conversations. Take with a grain of salt.

- Almost everyone mentioned **time** — but time isn't really the deepest pain. The deeper pain is **missing things they should have caught**. Multiple AEs described the bad outcome as "I didn't notice X until it was too late."
- **Trust** came up unprompted in three of five conversations. A wrong fact destroys trust quickly. AEs want the agent to err on the side of saying less.
- Two AEs (Lena, Sofia) explicitly want help surfacing what's *not* in their own notes — support tickets, recent usage signals, SDR activity. The unspoken inverse: don't waste their attention summarising what they already know.
- AEs are **not all the same**. Enterprise (Ines) has a very different shape from MM (Sofia, Lena, Thomas). Building one thing for both is unlikely to land well.
- Several AEs mentioned **competitive context** as a specific intent — they want to know which battlecard is relevant, not have to find it.
- **Adoption risk**: Marcus is sceptical. He'll try it once. If it doesn't earn its keep on the first interaction, he won't come back. He's representative of more AEs than we'd like.
